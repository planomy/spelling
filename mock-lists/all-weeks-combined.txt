# All 4 Weeks — Year 5 Renewable Energy Unit (40 words)
# Paste into SpellQuest to load all 4 weeks at once

1. energy - the power to make things work, move, or change
2. renewable - something that can be used again and again because it naturally replaces itself
3. non-renewable - a resource that runs out and cannot easily be replaced, like coal or oil
4. fossil-fuel - fuel formed from ancient plants and animals buried underground for millions of years
5. electricity - a form of energy that powers lights, devices, and machines
6. generate - to produce or create something, especially electricity
7. source - the place or thing where energy or power comes from
8. sustainable - able to continue for a long time without harming the environment
9. resource - something useful that people need, such as water, sunlight, or wind
10. consumption - the amount of energy or resources that people use
11. solar - relating to the sun and its energy
12. photovoltaic - describing panels that turn sunlight directly into electricity
13. panel - a flat device, often on a roof, that collects sunlight or converts it to power
14. turbine - a machine with spinning blades that uses moving air or water to make electricity
15. windmill - an old-fashioned structure that uses wind power, often to grind grain
16. blade - the long flat part of a turbine that catches the wind and spins
17. rotate - to turn around in a circle
18. convert - to change something from one form into another, such as sunlight into electricity
19. efficient - working well without wasting much energy
20. installation - the process of setting up equipment such as solar panels or wind turbines
21. hydroelectric - electricity made by using the force of moving water
22. dam - a wall built across a river to hold back water and create power
23. geothermal - heat energy that comes from deep inside the Earth
24. biomass - organic matter such as plants and waste used as a fuel source
25. combustion - the process of burning something to release heat energy
26. emission - gas or pollution released into the air, often from burning fuels
27. reservoir - a large stored supply of water held behind a dam
28. tidal - relating to the rise and fall of ocean tides, which can be used to generate power
29. decompose - to break down naturally over time, as plants and food waste do
30. methane - a gas produced when organic waste breaks down; it can be used as fuel
31. environment - the natural world around us, including air, water, land, and living things
32. carbon-dioxide - a gas released when fossil fuels are burned; it contributes to climate change
33. greenhouse - describing gases that trap heat in the atmosphere and warm the planet
34. climate - the usual weather patterns of a place over a long period of time
35. pollution - harmful substances that damage the air, water, or land
36. alternative - another choice or option, such as using renewable energy instead of coal
37. investigate - to find out facts by researching and asking questions
38. conclusion - the final part of a report where you sum up what you have found
39. paragraph - a group of sentences about one main idea in a report
40. subheading - a smaller title that organises information in a report
